# Rolling-element bearing and lubrication failure modes (technical reference)

*Paraphrased reference notes (license-clean). See ISO 15243 (rolling bearing damage
classification) and ISO 281 (dynamic load rating / L10 life).*

## Why bearings fail

Rolling-element bearings rarely reach their calculated **L10 fatigue life**; most are
cut short by a preventable cause. ISO 15243 groups bearing damage into six families:

1. **Subsurface/surface fatigue** — the "natural" end of life: spalling from repeated
   rolling contact stress. Shows as flaking on races/elements and rising vibration at
   defect frequencies.
2. **Wear** — abrasive (dirt ingress) or adhesive (insufficient lubricant film, metal-to-
   metal). Produces clearance growth, noise, and wear metal in the oil.
3. **Corrosion** — moisture/water (etching, rust) or fretting corrosion at fits where
   micro-movement occurs (reddish-brown oxide at the bore or housing).
4. **Electrical erosion** — current passage (e.g. VFD shaft currents) causes pitting and
   "fluting" / washboard marks; mitigate with shaft grounding rings or insulated bearings.
5. **Plastic deformation** — true brinelling (static overload/impact) or false brinelling
   (vibration while stationary, e.g. in transport); indentations at ball spacing.
6. **Fracture and cracking** — forced fits, overheating, or rough handling.

## Root causes you can act on

Studies attribute the majority of premature bearing failures to **lubrication problems
and contamination**, not fatigue. The actionable causes:

- **Lubrication** — wrong grease/oil, wrong quantity (over- and under-greasing both
  fail), depleted or contaminated lubricant, incompatible grease mixing.
- **Contamination** — dirt, water, and process ingress through worn seals.
- **Mounting** — misalignment, incorrect fit/clearance, impact during installation
  (always heat-mount or use proper tools; never hammer through the rolling elements).
- **Operating conditions** — overload, overspeed, overheating, stray current.

## Lubrication fundamentals

- **Right lubricant, right amount, right time, right place** — the four rights.
- **Over-greasing** raises churning friction and temperature, blows seals, and is a top
  cause of motor-bearing failure; relieve the drain plug and grease in small measured
  amounts on a condition/interval basis.
- **Cleanliness is reliability** — control ingress (seals, breathers/desiccant), filter
  and store oil clean, and treat oil analysis cleanliness codes (ISO 4406) as a leading
  indicator. A large share of hydraulic and bearing failures trace back to particle and
  water contamination.

## Diagnosis link

Bearing faults announce themselves early: rising broadband vibration, defect-frequency
peaks in the spectrum, ultrasonic friction before audible noise, heat on thermography,
and wear metals in oil. Catch them on the P-F curve and correct the *cause*
(lubrication, contamination, alignment) — not just the symptom.
