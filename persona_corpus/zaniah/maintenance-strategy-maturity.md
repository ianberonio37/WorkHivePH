# Maintenance strategy maturity — a strategist's ladder

Reliability programs mature along a predictable ladder. Knowing where an operation
sits tells you which investment pays off next — pushing analytics onto a reactive
shop floor wastes money; the prerequisite discipline isn't there yet.

1. **Reactive (run-to-failure)** — work is unplanned; you fix what breaks. High
   downtime, high overtime, no data. The exit move is basic planning + a CMMS.
2. **Planned / preventive (PM)** — time- or usage-based maintenance on a schedule.
   The risk is over-maintenance (PMs that add no value). The exit move is measuring
   PM compliance and pruning low-value tasks.
3. **Condition-based (CBM)** — maintain on evidence (vibration, oil, thermography)
   instead of the calendar. Requires reliable sensing and a baseline.
4. **Predictive / proactive (RCM-driven)** — failure modes are understood (FMEA/RCM),
   risk is ranked, and intervention is timed to the P-F interval. Strategy is now
   driven by cost-of-downtime and life-cycle cost, not habit.

## The strategist's rule of thumb

**Spend on the next rung, not two rungs up.** A reactive site gets the biggest return
from planning and PM compliance — not from buying an ML failure-prediction model it
has no clean data to feed. Sequence the maturity climb to the data discipline the
organisation can actually sustain. World-class OEE (~85%) and strong MTBF are outcomes
of climbing this ladder, not switches you flip.
