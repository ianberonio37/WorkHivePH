# Permit-to-work and safe isolation (shared reference)

*Paraphrased reference notes (license-clean). General good practice consistent with
DOLE OSHS and international permit-to-work systems.*

## Permit-to-work (PTW)

A permit-to-work is a formal, documented authorisation that controls high-risk
maintenance activities. It forces planning, communication, and accountability before
work starts. A PTW system typically covers:

- **Hot work** — welding, grinding, cutting, anything producing heat/sparks near
  combustibles.
- **Confined-space entry** — tanks, vessels, pits; requires gas testing, ventilation,
  attendant, and rescue plan.
- **Electrical / high-voltage work** — isolation and proving dead before contact.
- **Work at height**, **excavation**, **line/equipment breaking** (opening pressurised or
  product-filled systems), and **lifting** operations.

Each permit names the task, hazards, controls, the isolations required, the authorised
people, validity time, and a sign-off to start and to close. The permit is the bridge
between the risk assessment and the hands doing the work.

## Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) — the core of safe isolation

LOTO isolates hazardous energy so equipment cannot start while someone works on it.
Apply it to **every** energy source — electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic,
thermal, chemical, and stored (springs, capacitors, elevated parts). The sequence:

1. **Prepare / notify** — identify all energy sources; tell affected workers.
2. **Shut down** using the normal stopping procedure.
3. **Isolate** every source (open disconnects, close and chain valves, block movement).
4. **Lock and tag** each isolation point with the worker's own lock and tag.
5. **Release stored energy** — bleed pressure, discharge capacitors, block/lower gravity.
6. **Verify zero energy** — attempt a normal start (it must not run), then test with
   instruments. **The try-to-start verification is the step people skip — never skip it.**

## Non-negotiable rules

- **One worker, one lock, one key.** Each person applies and removes only their own lock;
  a group lockbox holds isolation keys when a crew works together.
- **A tag is not a lock** — a tag only warns; use a lock wherever a device accepts one.
- **The AI Companion never authorises a permit or a LOTO scope.** Safety decisions —
  permit issuance, isolation scope, confined-space entry, proving dead — are always made
  and signed by a competent, authorised person. Treat any AI guidance as input to that
  human decision, cross-checked against the OEM manual and DOLE OSHS, never a substitute.
