# Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) — safety basics

Lockout/Tagout is the discipline of isolating hazardous energy so equipment cannot
start while someone is working on it. It applies to all energy sources — electrical,
mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal, chemical, and stored (springs, capacitors,
elevated parts).

## The core sequence

1. **Prepare / notify** — identify all energy sources; tell affected workers.
2. **Shut down** the equipment using the normal stopping procedure.
3. **Isolate** every energy source (open disconnects, close valves, block movement).
4. **Lock and tag** each isolation point with the worker's own lock and a tag.
5. **Release stored energy** — bleed pressure, discharge capacitors, block/relieve gravity.
6. **Verify zero energy** — attempt a normal start (it must not run), then test with
   instruments. *Try-to-start is the step people skip — never skip it.*

## Key rules

- **One worker, one lock, one key.** Each person applies their own lock; only they
  remove it. A group lockbox holds the isolation keys when a crew works together.
- A **tag is not a lock** — a tag alone only warns; it does not physically prevent
  energization. Use a lock wherever a device accepts one.
- LOTO is required before any servicing where unexpected start-up or energy release
  could injure a worker. It is the foundation of permit-to-work for maintenance.
