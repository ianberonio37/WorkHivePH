The Industrial Reality

Filipino workers are skilled.
The system is failing them.

Six systemic failures compound into national economic loss. A technician who prevents ten failures receives less credit than one who fixes a single breakdown. The problems aren't caused by individual incompetence. They're caused by broken systems.

Fragmentation

The maintenance system doesn't connect to inventory. Inventory doesn't connect to purchasing. Every boundary requires a human to carry information, manually, imperfectly.

Knowledge Loss

When a technician retires, 12 years of expertise disappears entirely. Replacing that knowledge costs ₱800K to ₱2.4M in lost productivity per transition.

Reactive Processes

An unplanned failure costs 4 to 8 times more than the same repair done preventively. The warnings were there. No system was listening.

Skills Invisibility

Organizations can't deploy their best people to their most critical problems because they don't know what their people can actually do. Jobs are assigned by availability, not competence.
