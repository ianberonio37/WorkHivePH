## 1. HOOK VARIETY — retire "problem at 3 AM"; there are 8+ hook types

The first cuts opened every variant on the same problem-agitation ("It's 3 AM, the line stops"). Research says variety wins and problem-agitation is only ONE of many; an empowerment/memory product is better served by transformation, curiosity, and belief-flip hooks.

| Hook type | Shape | Fits WorkHive as |
|---|---|---|
| **Transformation / outcome** | hint the after-state ("here's what happened when…") | "Imagine never losing a fix again" |
| **Curiosity / open-loop** | pose a question the video answers | "What if the answer was already in your pocket?" |
| **Belief-flip / unpopular truth** | lead with a bold contrarian claim | "Your best engineer's knowledge shouldn't walk out the door" |
| **POV / relatable moment** | "you are [relatable situation]…" | "You fixed this exact fault last year. Can you remember how?" |
| **Face close-up** | a human face in frame — natural attention magnet | supports the "animated AI people" ask (§2) |
| **Shock-stat** | one surprising real number | only if the number is TRUE + relatable (not fabricated) |
| **Loop-completion** | last frame flows into the first so replays count as satisfaction (YouTube 2025-26 signal) | end card visually mirrors the open |
| **Hook-then-reward** | open a loop, pay it off later | "Here's how →" then the tour |

Rules that still hold: **3-second window** (avg attention ~8.2s), **visual hook in the first frame before a word**, **silent-first** (85% watch muted; captions + visuals carry it — boosts completion ~40%).

Sources: [vidIQ — 18 hooks that stop the scroll (2026)](https://vidiq.com/blog/post/viral-video-hooks-youtube-shorts/) · [DriveEditor — 2025 hook trends](https://driveeditor.com/blog/trends-short-form-video-hooks) · [Fresh Faced Marketing — better hooks 2025](https://www.freshfacedmarketing.co.uk/marketing-blog/how-to-write-better-hooks-for-short-form-video-in-2025) · [CreatorsJet — 7 viral hook frameworks](https://www.creatorsjet.com/blog/7-viral-hook-frameworks-for-short-form-video-creators) · [Levitate Media — short-form best practices 2026](https://levitatemedia.com/learn/mastering-short-form-video-production-best-practices-and-marketing-tips)

## 2. ANIMATED CHARACTERS / "AI PEOPLE" — a relatable guide, not just UI mockups

Ian: "add some visual AI people like they are animating." **Clarified (2026-07-02): the platform screenshots are LOVED and stay — the character is a SUPPLEMENT layered alongside them, not a replacement. The video "feels lacking" without an animated AI presence.** So: keep the phone/product-hero mockups exactly as they are, and ADD an animated AI-companion + worker character that appears with them (in the hook, and beside the phone during the tour). Research strongly backs the character: character-driven explainers add a **relatable focal point + emotional connection**, which **increases watch time, retention, and memorability**, and a consistent character becomes part of **brand identity**.

- The character should act as a **GUIDE** (StoryBrand: the worker is the hero, the AI/WorkHive is the guide) — a friendly AI presence that walks the worker through, not the star.
- **Relatability** comes from universal expressions + a universal challenge the audience shares (forgetting, being overwhelmed), NOT a specific named person.
- **Consistency** across cuts/posters = the character reads as WorkHive's mascot/identity.
- Faces draw the eye (the "face close-up" hook, §1) — an animated AI figure in the hook is a strong pattern-interrupt.

For our **pure-Python Pillow+ffmpeg engine** ([[feedback_build_own_minimal_dependencies]]): implement as a simple, on-brand animated figure — a worker + a glowing "AI companion" orb/avatar with a face, eased spring entrances, a gentle idle bob, and a "thinking → answer" beat — NOT photoreal, NOT a new dependency. A minimal, warm, geometric character in the brand palette (navy + orange + aurora) beats no character.

## 3. MESSAGING AN AI-MEMORY / SECOND-BRAIN / SAVE-TIME PRODUCT

WorkHive's "build your own AI / access your memory" is the same category as personal-AI / "second brain" — and that field has a clear messaging playbook:

- **Cognitive partnership, NOT "a tool you visit."** Frame it as something that *accompanies you and accumulates understanding over time* — an extension of the worker, not an app.
- **Associative recall is the hero benefit:** "surfaces the note from three months ago you forgot was relevant." For WorkHive: *"you fixed this before — WorkHive remembers how."*
- **Cumulative learning:** it *learns from your edits / your hive's history* and compounds — "the more you use it, the more it's yours." This is the differentiator vs a generic chatbot (ChatGPT knows the world; your WorkHive AI knows YOUR plant).
- **Reduced cognitive load / time saved:** it finds the connection you'd have missed; the remembering is done for you.
- **Accessible sovereignty:** control + privacy (your hive's data stays yours) with consumer-grade ease. Worth a line given industrial data sensitivity.

## 4. CONCRETE, NOT JARGON — the TX-001 / Cabuyao lesson (the nuance that resolves the critique)

The critique wasn't "be less specific." Research shows **concrete, specific language BUILDS trust**: increasing concreteness ~5.6% raised customer satisfaction ~8.9% (JCR); specific numbers make a promise tangible and credible; concreteness signals you understand the customer's real need. The failure was the WRONG KIND of specific:

- **TX-001 / P-204B = internal JARGON.** Cryptic asset tags mean nothing to a viewer → jargon alienates, confuses, and pushes people away. (This is the same failure as buzzwords.)
- **Cabuyao = FABRICATION.** Inventing a specific place is untrue AND narrows a nationwide audience to one city. Fabricated specifics erode the trust that real specifics build.
- **The good kind of concrete = relatable + TRUE:** a universal moment ("the fix you did last year"), the real 8 tools by name, "every lesson kept," a real time saved. Concrete and vivid, but nothing invented and nothing only-an-insider-knows.

**Rule:** be concrete with RELATABLE, TRUE detail; never cryptic internal identifiers, never invented places/people/numbers. Abstract for the vision line, concrete for the "here's how it helps you" lines.

Sources: [Behavioral Scientist / Jonah Berger — concrete vs abstract](https://behavioralscientist.org/the-magic-of-knowing-when-to-use-concrete-vs-abstract-language/) · [JCR (Oxford) — concrete language & satisfaction](https://academic.oup.com/jcr/article/47/5/787/5873524) · [InsideBE — concrete language is memorable](https://insidebe.com/articles/make-your-copy-memorable/) · [Digital.gov — avoid jargon (plain language)](https://digital.gov/guides/plain-language/principles/avoid-jargon) · [Gavel Intl — 12 words that hurt copy](https://www.gavelintl.com/12-words-that-hurt-marketing-copy-and-efforts/)
