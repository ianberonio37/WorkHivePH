Frequently asked questions

What is the role of BMS in ensuring building safety?
BMS plays a critical role in ensuring building safety by monitoring and controlling various building systems, including life-safety and security systems. In the Philippines, the Department of Labor and Employment's Occupational Safety and Health Standards (DOLE OSHS) require building owners to implement safety measures to protect occupants.
How often should BMS equipment be maintained?
BMS equipment should be maintained regularly, ideally on a monthly basis, to ensure optimal performance and extend equipment lifespan. The Philippine Electrical Code (IIEE Code) recommends regular maintenance of electrical equipment, including BMS components.
What are the benefits of using WorkHive's Hive for BMS and facilities maintenance?
WorkHive's Hive provides a centralized platform for managing BMS and facilities maintenance, enabling multi-tenant facility teams to streamline communication, scheduling, and resource allocation. This leads to increased efficiency, reduced downtime, and improved tenant satisfaction.
What are the PEZA-specific compliance requirements for BMS and facilities maintenance?
PEZA-registered buildings must comply with relevant Philippine regulations and standards, including the DOLE OSHS and the IIEE Code. Building owners and facility managers must ensure that BMS and facilities maintenance meet these requirements to maintain PEZA registration.
How can I ensure that my BMS and facilities maintenance team is properly trained?
Building owners and facility managers can ensure that their BMS and facilities maintenance team is properly trained by providing regular training and upskilling programs. The International Institute of Industrial Engineering (IIIE) and the Society for Maintenance and Reliability Professionals (SMRP) offer training and certification programs for maintenance professionals.
What are the consequences of non-compliance with PEZA regulations?
Non-compliance with PEZA regulations can result in penalties, fines, and even cancellation of PEZA registration. Building owners and facility managers must ensure that their BMS and facilities maintenance meet PEZA requirements to avoid these consequences.
